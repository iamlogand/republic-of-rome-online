## Ally Deserts {#event-ally-deserts}

- Roman allies are wavering. All battles fought this turn with an even result on 3d6 will result in a temporary increase to the War card's strength for this turn equal to the result on the black die. This increase is applied after any multipliers for Matching Wars.

### Dark blue side: Roman Auxiliary Deserts

- Roman troops are shaken. All battles fought this turn with an even result on 3d6 will result in a temporary increase to the War card's strength for this turn equal to the result on the white dice. This increase is applied after any multipliers for Matching Wars.

## Allied Enthusiasm {#event-allied-enthusiasm}

- State receives 50T in the Revenue Phase.

### Dark blue side: Extreme Allied Enthusiasm

- State receives 75T in the Revenue Phase.
- Further Allied Enthusiasm events have no effect.

## Barbarian Raids {#event-barbarian-raids}

- At the start of the next Revenue Phase, the Governor of each Frontier Province must make a Raid roll of 2d6 + Military Force of the Province to avoid being Overrun.
- Military Force = Military Strength + 2/Garrison Legion + MIL of Governor.
- A result > 15 is required to avoid being Overrun.
- An Overrun Province loses all income, reverts to Undeveloped if Developed and may not roll for Development this turn. Mortality chits equal to the black die of the Raid roll are drawn for the Governor and the Province loses that number of Garrisoned Legions if available. If the Governor is captured, the Ransom must be paid before the next Forum Phase or he is killed.
- (Affected by) Advanced Provincial Wars Rule

### Dark blue side: Barbarian Raids Increase

- At the start of the next Revenue Phase, the Governor of each Frontier Province must make a Raid roll of 2d6 + Military Force of the Province to avoid being Overrun.
- Military Force = Military Strength + 2/Garrison Legion + MIL of Governor.
- A result > 17 is required to avoid being Overrun.
- An Overrun Province loses all income, reverts to Undeveloped if Developed and may not roll for Development this turn. Mortality chits equal to the black die of the Raid roll are drawn for the Governor and the Province loses that number of Garrisoned Legions if available. If the Governor is captured, the Ransom must be paid before the next Forum Phase or he is killed.
- (Affected by) Advanced Provincial Wars Rule

## Drought {#event-drought}

- Creates or worsens Drought conditions.

### Dark blue side: Severe Drought

- Worsens Drought conditions.
- Additional Drought events further worsen Drought conditions. Use the Drought tokes to keep track of the number of drought effects in play.

## Enemy's Ally Deserts {#event-enemys-ally-deserts}

- All battles fought this turn with an odd result on 3d6 will result in a temporary decrease to the War's Strength for this turn equal to the result on the black die.
- This decrease is applied after any multipliers for Matching Wars. The minimum Strength it can be lowered to is 0.

### Dark blue side: Enemy Mercenaries Desert

- All battles fought this turn with an odd result on 3d6 will result in a temporary decrease to the War's Strength for this turn equal to the result on the white dice.
- This decrease is applied after any multipliers for Matching Wars. The minimum Strength it can be lowered to is 0.

## Enemy Leader Dies {#event-enemy-leader-dies}

- At the end of the Forum Phase, the HRAO chooses one enemy Leader in play to be discarded.
- Not applicable to Rebel Senators.

### Dark blue side: Enemy Sues for Peace

- At the end of the Forum Phase, the HRAO chooses one enemy Leader in play to be discarded. Disheartened by the loss of their Leader, the largest current Matching War of that Leader sues for peace. The State receives half of any Spoils of War (rounded down) and shuffles the War into the top six cards of the deck.
- Not applicable to Rebel Senators.

## Epidemic {#event-epidemic}

- Draw six Mortality Chits.
- Only those Senators currently in Rome can be killed.

### Dark blue side: Foreign Epidemic

- Draw six Mortality Chits.
- Only the first Governor, Proconsul, Captive or Rebel drawn who is currently away from Rome is killed.
- Further Epidemic events are resolved as Foreign Epidemic event.

## Evil Omens {#event-evil-omens}

- The State Treasury must immediately pay 20T for sacrifices and Temple repair and a −1 penalty is applies to EVERY die or dice roll except for Initiative and further rolls on the Events table. (Exception: +1 to all Persuasion Attempts.) In the case of Provincial Spoils and State Income subtract one from the total income — not from the die roll.
- Pontifex Maximus Advanced Rule

### Dark blue side: More Evil Omens

- Only one Evil Omens sacrifice of 20T is required per turn but, if additional Evil Omens event(s) ae rolled in the same turn, penalties to die or dice rolls, excluding Initiative rolls, are increased by an additional −1 (or +1 in the case of Persuasion Attempts). Provincial Spoils and State income are also reduced by 1 per additional Evil Omens Event.

## Internal Disorder {#event-internal-disorder}

- All Undeveloped Provinces have no Revenue in the next Revenue Phase and may not make an Improvement attempt. The Governor of every Undeveloped Province must make an Order roll of 1d6 +1 per Garrison Legion at the start of the Revenue Phase to prevent a revolt.
- A result >4 is required to avoid the Revolt.
- A successful Revolt kills the Governor and his Garrison and moves the Province card to the Forum as an Active War. A Province in Revolt like this should be considered a War card in all respects with its Strengths being equal to the Province's printed base Land and Fleet Strength.
- (Affected by) Advanced Provincial Wars Rule

### Dark blue side: Increased Internal Disorder

- All Undeveloped Provinces have no Revenue in the next Revenue Phase and may not make an Improvement attempt. The Governor of every Undeveloped Province must make an Order roll of 1d6 +1 per Garrison Legion at the start of the Revenue Phase to prevent a revolt.
- A result >5 is required to avoid the Revolt.
- A successful Revolt kills the Governor and his Garrison and moves the Province card to the Forum as an Active War. A Province in Revolt like this should be considered a War card in all respects with its Strengths being equal to the Province's printed base Land and Fleet Strength.
- (Affected by) Advanced Provincial Wars Rule

## Manpower Shortage {#event-manpower-shortage}

- The cost of raising new Legions and Fleets is increased to 20T each.
- Place the Manpower Shortage token in play to reflect the cost.

### Dark blue side: Increased Manpower Shortage

- The cost of raising new Legions and Fleets is further increased by 10T each.
- Additional Manpower Shortage events further increase costs by 10T each. Use the Manpower Shortage tokens to reflect the current cost of raising Legions and Fleets.

## Mob Violence {#event-mob-violence}

- Draw Mortality Chits equal to the current Unrest Level.
- Only those Senators currently in Rome whose Popularity is less than the current Unrest Level can be killed.

### Dark blue side: More Mob Violence

- Draw Mortality Chits equal to the current Unrest LEvel + 1d6.
- Only those Senators currently in Rome whose Popularity is less than the current Unrest Level can be killed.
- Further Mob Violence events are resolved as More Mob Violence events.

## Natural Disaster {#event-natural-disaster}

- The State Treasury must immediately pay 50T for relief. Roll 1d6 and destroy the corresponding Concession as follows:
  - 1–2 Destroy Mining
  - 3–4 Destroy Harbor Fees
  - 5 Destroy Armaments
  - 6 Destroy Ship Building
- Destroyed Concessions are returned to the Curia.
  If the rolled Concession is already destroyed or not in play, there is no additional effect.

### Dark blue side: Widespread Natural Disaster

- Only one Natural Disaster requiring a 50T payment may occur per turn but, if additional Natural Disaster event(s) are rolled in the same turn, another Concession must be destroyed corresponding to the roll of a 1d6 as per the Natural Disaster event.

## New Alliance {#event-new-alliance}

- A neutral state sides with Rome, shifting the balance of power. The enemy sues for peace! Must be applies to a War or Revolt of HRAO's choice at the end of the Senate Phase. State collects half of any Spoils of War (rounded down). Shuffle the War into the top six cards of the deck. If the selected War is a Pretender Province the Revolt is completely defeated.
- The chosen War loses any "Naval Victory" marker if shuffled back into the deck.

### Dark blue side: Another New Alliance

- Yet another neutral state sides with Rome on the same war. The enemy is desperate for peace! Must be applied to a War of the HRAO's choice at the end of the Senate Phase. State collects all Spoils of War and the War is discarded. This may not be applied to a Revolt.
- When a War is discarded in this manner, provinces are still created.

## Pretender Emerges {#event-pretender-emerges}

- When the next Active War card appears, a randomly selected Province immediately revolts. An immediate battle between the Governor plus any Garrison Legions occur. Any result except Victory for the Governor results in the death of the Governor and all surviving Garrisoned Legions immediately return to Rome. Move the province card to the Forum as an active Revolt. A Province in Revolt like this should be considered a War card in all respects with its Strength being equal to the Province's printed Fleet and Land Strength.
- Remains in play until the Revolt occurs.
- (Affected by) Advanced Provincial Wars Rule

### Dark blue side: Pretender Victorious

- When the next Active War card appears, a randomly selected Province immediately revolts. The Governor and any Garrisoned Legions are killed outright. Move the Province card to the Forum as an active Revolt. A Province in Revolt like this should be considered a War card in all respects with its Strength being equal to the Province's printed Fleet and Land Strength.
- Remains in play until the Revolt occurs.
- (Affected by) Advanced Provincial Wars Rule

## Refuge {#event-refuge}

- The next Enemy Leader killed by a Victory will instead be given Refuge by the next War card drawn.
- That Leader may remain in play and aid that War but is discarded when that War is defeated.
- Remains in play until used.

### Dark blue side: Rise from Refuge

- The next Enemy Leader killed by a Victory will instead be given Refuge. That leader remains in play and activates all current Wars adding his strength to the final strength of all the Wars. Multiple activated Matching Wars must still follow standard Activation and Imminent Wars rules.
- The Leader does not activate or aid future Wars drawn from this point on.
- The Leader is only discarded when the last War he aids is discarded.
- Remains in play until used.

## Rhodian Alliance {#event-rhodian-alliance}

- If no current Wars require Fleets this event has no effect and is immediately discarded. The State receives 8 free fleets. Only maintenance costs in future Revenue Phases apply. 8 fleets (or all Rome has, whichever is fewer) must be disbanded upon the defeat of the current War (at the end of the Combat Phase) requiring the most Fleets (Fleet Support + Modified Fleet Strength). This card can be rejected by a vote of the Senate in the upcoming Senate Phase (after vacant Provinces have been filled) to avoid maintenance costs only on the turn of the card's appearance. The card remains in the Forum until either rejected or the defeated War condition is met. If there are two wars requiring the most Fleets then defeat of one of the Wars will end this event. The Fleets provided by this event count towards the 25 Fleet limit.

### Dark blue side: Increased Rhodian Involvement

- If no current Wars require Fleets this event has no effect and is immediately discarded.
- This event is identical to the Rhodian Maritime Alliance except the State receives 12 free fleets.

## Storm at Sea {#event-storm-at-sea}

- 2d6 Roman Fleets of the HRAO's choice (deployed or not deployed but excluding Provincial Fleets) are eliminated (up to a maximum of those currently existing). There is no effect on Legions although some may have to be withdrawn to Italy for lack of support if sufficient additional fleets cannot be raised during the Senate Phase.
- This event has no effect on the construction of new Fleets.

### Dark blue side: Another Storm at Sea

- 2d6 Roman Fleets of the HRAO's choice (deployed or not deployed but excluding Provincial Fleets) are eliminated (up to a maximum of those currently existing). There is no effect on Legions although some may have to be withdrawn to Italy for lack of support if sufficient additional fleets cannot be raised during the Senate Phase.
- This event has no effect on the construction of new Fleets.
- Additional Storms at Sea events may still occur.

## Trial of Verres - 70 BC {#event-trial-of-verres}

- Successful Prosecutions of corrupt Governors discourages Provincial misgovernment. During the next Revenue Phase the State Income from every Province is increased by 3T. Governor Provincial Spoils are decreased by the same amount. Provincial Spoils can only be reduced to a minimum of 0, the State does not pay more as a result of this event.

### Dark blue side: Another Corruption Trial

- Further Prosecutions of corrupt Governors discourages Provincial misgovernment. During the next Revenue Phase the State Income from every Province is increased by 5T. Governor Provincial Spoils are decreased by the same amount. Provincial Spoils can only be reduced to a minimum of 0, the State does not pay more as a result of this event.
