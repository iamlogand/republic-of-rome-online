# VII. Index and Glossary

[← Rules Index](README.md)

## INDEX AND GLOSSARY {#7}

### A

**Abstain:** Not voting for or against a Proposal. ([1.09.12](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.12), [1.09.14](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.14))

**Active Forces boxes:** The area on the board that shows the active Legions and Fleets in Italy.

**Active Wars:** Wars that are currently attacking Rome. ([1.07.33](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.33)). A Civil War counts as an Active War unless a Rebel has achieved one of his Winning Conditions (1 or 2 from [1.12.2](../01-basic-game/1.12-victory-conditions.md#1.12.2)).

**Adequate Force:** In a solitaire or two-player game it is the number of Legions or Fleets equal to the Strength of the opposing War. ([4.05.31](../04-solitaire-two-player.md#4.05.31))

**Advocate:** A consenting Senator appointed by the Accused to defend against a specific Prosecution. ([2.05](../02-advanced-game/2.05-advocates.md#2.05)) (Trial: [2.05.3](../02-advanced-game/2.05-advocates.md#2.05.3))

**Aligned Senator:** An in-play Senator controlled by a player.

**Alternate History Scenario:** A Scenario which uses all the decks shuffled into one. ([3.05.1](../03-scenarios/3.05-alternative-history.md#3.05.1))

**Army:** The Legions and/or Provincial Armies assigned to (or following) a Commander or Rebel Governor.

**Assassinations:** ([1.09.7](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.7)) (Resolution: [1.09.72](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.72)) (Effects on Senate: [1.09.721](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.721)) (Assassin card: [1.09.7](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.7)) (N/A Public Agreement: [1.04.3](../01-basic-game/1.04-game-terms-and-concepts.md#1.04.3)) (Bodyguards: [1.09.73](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.73))

### B

**Bank:** The public supply of all unused markers and chits; not to be confused with the State Treasury or available Force Pool.

**Bankruptcy:** A condition that occurs when Rome cannot pay its Debts. ([1.06.53](../01-basic-game/1.06-revenue-phase.md#1.06.53))

**Base Province Strength:** The intrinsic printed Land and/or Naval Strength of a Province. The Base Province Strength does not include Garrisons and Provincial Forces. ([1.09.646](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.646))

**Bodyguards:** Intrigue cards that help defend against Assassinations. ([1.09.73](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.73))

### C

**Captive:** A Senator who has been captured. ([1.10.71](../01-basic-game/1.10-combat-phase.md#1.10.71))

**Censor:** A prior Consul that conducts Prosecutions. ([1.09.4](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.4)) (Death Consequences: [1.09.41](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.41))

**Civil War:** When one or more Senators Revolt against the State. (Declaration: [1.11.3](../01-basic-game/1.11-revolution-phase.md#1.11.3)) (Maintenance: [1.11.35](../01-basic-game/1.11-revolution-phase.md#1.11.35)) (Resolution: [1.11.37](../01-basic-game/1.11-revolution-phase.md#1.11.37)) (Winning Condition: [1.12.2](../01-basic-game/1.12-victory-conditions.md#1.12.2), [1.12.3](../01-basic-game/1.12-victory-conditions.md#1.12.3)), (Rebel Governors: [2.03](../02-advanced-game/2.03-rebel-governors.md#2.03))

**Combat Phase:** ([1.10](../01-basic-game/1.10-combat-phase.md#1.10))

**Commander:** Any Senator in control of a military Force. (Commander Death: [1.10.7](../01-basic-game/1.10-combat-phase.md#1.10.7)) (Capture: [1.10.71](../01-basic-game/1.10-combat-phase.md#1.10.71))

**Concession:** Economic consideration cards owned by a particular Senator. ([1.07.32](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.32)) (Revenue Collection: [1.06.12](../01-basic-game/1.06-revenue-phase.md#1.06.12)) (Corruption: [1.06.12](../01-basic-game/1.06-revenue-phase.md#1.06.12)) (Reviving: [1.07.8](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.8)) (Proposal: [1.09.61](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.61)) (Destroying: [1.07.321](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.321)) (Playing: [1.11.1](../01-basic-game/1.11-revolution-phase.md#1.11.1))

**Consul For Life:** Usually the winner of the Game. (Election: [1.09.821](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.821)) (Appointment: [1.09.822](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.822)) (Resolution: [1.09.823](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.823))

**Consuls:** The Rome and Field Consuls. ([1.09.2](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.2))

**Contributions:** Whenever a Senator makes a donation to the State Treasury. ([1.06.52](../01-basic-game/1.06-revenue-phase.md#1.06.52))

**Crisis:** In a solitaire or two-player game it is when Rome is faced with 3 or more Active Wars, or a War with Strength > 15, or a Rebel. ([4.05.3](../04-solitaire-two-player.md#4.05.3)) (List: [4.05.3](../04-solitaire-two-player.md#4.05.3)) (Offices: [4.05.34](../04-solitaire-two-player.md#4.05.34))

**Curia:** The three-space area below the Forum where destroyed Concessions and dead Family cards wait to be revived and Leaders without Wars await aging die rolls. ([1.07.8](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.8))

### D

**Dangerous War:** In a solitaire or two-player game, this is a War that has Matching Wars waiting to be drawn, regardless of whether or not it is currently Active. ([4.05.32](../04-solitaire-two-player.md#4.05.32))

**Death Consequences:** ([1.05.3](../01-basic-game/1.05-mortality-phase.md#1.05.3))

**Debits:** Expenses that must be paid out of the State Treasury in the Revenue Phase. ([1.06.53](../01-basic-game/1.06-revenue-phase.md#1.06.53))

**Defeats:** ([1.10.2](../01-basic-game/1.10-combat-phase.md#1.10.2)) (Provincial War: [2.02.412](../02-advanced-game/2.02-provincial-wars.md#2.02.412)) (Rebel Province: [2.03.92](../02-advanced-game/2.03-rebel-governors.md#2.03.92))

**Deployment:** A proposal to send/recall a Commander with Legions/Fleets to fight a War. ([1.09.64](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.64)) (Minimum Force: [1.09.643](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.643)) (Recall: [1.09.644](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.644)) (Garrisons: [1.09.646](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.646)) (Reinforcements: [1.09.647](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.647))

**Dictator:** Always the HRAO, appointed/elected only in times of dire military emergency. ([1.09.3](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.3)) (Deployment Priority: [1.09.641](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.641))

**Disaster:** An unmodified battle roll result which leads to the loss of half of Rome's Forces. ([1.10.21](../01-basic-game/1.10-combat-phase.md#1.10.21))

**Disbandment:** Disbanding an Allied Veteran Legion. ([2.04.2](../02-advanced-game/2.04-legionary-disbandment-recall.md#2.04.2)) (Recall: [2.04.3](../02-advanced-game/2.04-legionary-disbandment-recall.md#2.04.3))

**Dominant Player:** In a two-player game, the Dominant Player is the player making the decisions. ([4.02.2](../04-solitaire-two-player.md#4.02.2))

**drm:** Die Roll Modifier.

**Drought Effect:** An Event or War that increases the Unrest by 1 in the Population Phase. Cumulative. ([1.08.12](../01-basic-game/1.08-population-phase.md#1.08.12))

### E

**Early Republic Scenario:** A Scenario which uses the white deck. ([3.01](../03-scenarios/3.01-early-republic.md#3.01))

**Era Ends Card:** An Event card that signifies that the Game is over at the end of the current Forum Phase. ([1.12.2](../01-basic-game/1.12-victory-conditions.md#1.12.2))

**Events:** ([1.07.21](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.21)) (Removal: [1.07.1](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.1))

**Evil Omens:** An Event that subtracts 1 from most rolls. (Pontifex: [2.01.7](../02-advanced-game/2.01-pontifex-maximus.md#2.01.7))

**Extended Scenario:** A scenario which uses decks in sequenced order (white > gray > red). ([3.04.1](../03-scenarios/3.04-extended-scenario.md#3.04.1))

### F

**Faction:** A set of Senators that are controlled by a player. (Elimination: [1.05.4](../01-basic-game/1.05-mortality-phase.md#1.05.4))

**Faction cards:** Any card with red text. These cards are kept hidden when drawn. These include Statesmen, Concession, Law, Intrigue and some Event cards. (Hand Limit: [1.11.2](../01-basic-game/1.11-revolution-phase.md#1.11.2)) (Exchanging: [1.04.5](../01-basic-game/1.04-game-terms-and-concepts.md#1.04.5))

**Faction Dominance:** In a solitaire or two-player game this is the current rank of each Faction in terms of overall vote totals. ([4.05.2](../04-solitaire-two-player.md#4.05.2))

**Faction Leader:** (Assassination Implications: [1.09.74](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.74)), (Persuasion Attempt Immunity: [1.07.4](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.4)), (Death Consequences: [1.05.3](../01-basic-game/1.05-mortality-phase.md#1.05.3)) (Changing: [1.07.7](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.7))

**Faction Treasury:** Talents kept hidden in the Faction Treasury boxes. ([1.06.31](../01-basic-game/1.06-revenue-phase.md#1.06.31))

**Family Senator:** A Senator with black text. ([1.07.311](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.311)) (Revival: [1.07.8](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.8))

**Field Consul:** Usually the principal Military Commander of Rome. ([1.09.2](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.2)) (Deployment Priority: [1.09.641](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.641))

**Fleet:** Naval Forces of Rome or a Rebel. (Recruitment: [1.09.63](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.63)) (Maintenance: [1.06.53](../01-basic-game/1.06-revenue-phase.md#1.06.53)) (Rebel Maintenance: [1.06.2](../01-basic-game/1.06-revenue-phase.md#1.06.2))

**Fleet Support:** A required minimum number of Fleets that must be present to even attempt to fight a Land Battle. ([1.10.11](../01-basic-game/1.10-combat-phase.md#1.10.11))

**Fleet:** (Maintenance: [1.06.52](../01-basic-game/1.06-revenue-phase.md#1.06.52)), (Rebel Maintenance: [1.06.2](../01-basic-game/1.06-revenue-phase.md#1.06.2))

**Force:** A collection of Legion and/or Fleet markers. ([1.09.63](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.63))

**Force Pool:** The space on the board where unbuilt Legions and Fleets are kept.

**Forum:** The 12 space location on the board depicting the map of the Mediterranean. Events, Concessions, and Unaligned Senators are displayed here. The spaces for Active Wars, Unprosecuted Wars, Imminent Wars, and Inactive Wars are also part of the Forum.

**Forum cards:** Any card with a black text. Thes cards are always revealed when drawn from the deck. These include Families, Wars and Leaders. ([1.07.3](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.3))

**Forum Deck:** The constructed deck that players draw from. ([3.01.4](../03-scenarios/3.01-early-republic.md#3.01.4), [3.02.2](../03-scenarios/3.02-middle-republic.md#3.02.2), [3.03.2](../03-scenarios/3.03-late-republic.md#3.03.2))

**Forum Phase:** ([1.07](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07))

**Frontier Provinces:** Provinces with underscored names susceptible to Barbarian Raids Events. ([1.09.6461](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.6461))

### G

**Game End:** ([1.12.1](../01-basic-game/1.12-victory-conditions.md#1.12.1), [1.12.2](../01-basic-game/1.12-victory-conditions.md#1.12.2) and [1.12.3](../01-basic-game/1.12-victory-conditions.md#1.12.3))

**Garrison:** Legions assigned to a Province under the Command of a Governor. ([1.09.646](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.646))

**Governor:** A particular Senator assigned to control/protect a Province. (Provincial Spoils: [1.06.13](../01-basic-game/1.06-revenue-phase.md#1.06.13)) (Return: [1.06.6](../01-basic-game/1.06-revenue-phase.md#1.06.6)) (Election: [1.09.5](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.5)) (Against Provincial War: [2.02.4](../02-advanced-game/2.02-provincial-wars.md#2.02.4)) (Rebel: [2.03.2](../02-advanced-game/2.03-rebel-governors.md#2.03.2))

**Gracch/Licinii Brothers:** Statesmen pairs (brothers) that can both be in play at the same time. (Death Resolution: [1.05.22](../01-basic-game/1.05-mortality-phase.md#1.05.22))

### H

**HRAO:** (Highest Ranking Available Official): The first official on the following list present in Rome: Dictator, Rome Consul Field Consul, Censor, Master of Horse, Pontifex Maximus, and followed by any Senator in Rome with the most Influence. ([1.09.11](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.11))

### I

**Imminent War:** An Inactive Matching War waiting to become an Active War. ([1.07.3321](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.3321))

**Inactive War:** A War that does not cost the State Talents and does not count against Rome for Losing Conditions. ([1.07.331](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.331))

**Influence:** A number indicating the current prestige of a Senator. It can never drop below zero. ([1.07.31](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.31))

**Initial Faction Phase:** The time at which players can play their Faction cards before the Game starts. ([3.01.9](../03-scenarios/3.01-early-republic.md#3.01.9))

**Initiative:** A four-step player Sequence within the Forum Phase executed by each player, one player at a time. ([1.07.2](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.2)) (Bidding on Initiatives: [1.07.22](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.22))

**Intrigue card:** A Faction card that allows a special ability. ([1.07.35](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.35))

### K

**Knight:** A member of the commercial class that gives his controlling Senator 1 Talent Revenue and 1 additional vote. (Revenue Bonus: [1.06.12](../01-basic-game/1.06-revenue-phase.md#1.06.12)) (Attracting: [1.07.5](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.5)) (Pressuring: [1.07.51](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.51))

### L

**Land Battle:** A battle fought using Legions and/or Provincial Armies, sometimes requiring Supporting Fleets. ([1.10.11](../01-basic-game/1.10-combat-phase.md#1.10.11))

**Land Bill:** A proposal to lower the Unrest and award Popularity at a cost applied to the State Treasury. ([1.09.62](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.62)) (Repeal: [1.09.621](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.621)) (Assassination Effect: [1.09.623](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.623))

**Late Republic Scenario:** Uses the red deck. (Setup: [3.03](../03-scenarios/3.03-late-republic.md#3.03))

**Laws:** A type of Faction card that, when played, changes the Game rules. ([1.07.36](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.36)) (Passing Laws Advanced Rule: [2.06](../02-advanced-game/2.06-passing-laws.md#2.06))

**Leaders:** Enemy commanders associated with Wars. ([1.07.34](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.34)) (Aging Die Rolls: [1.07.8](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.8))

**Legate:** A virtual official that acts as a proxy allowing a Governor to manage a Province remotely while the Vatinian Law is in effect. ([1.13.1](../01-basic-game/1.13-additional-card-notes.md#1.13.1))

**Legion:** The Land Forces of Rome. (Recruitment: [1.09.63](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.63)) (Maintenance: [1.06.52](../01-basic-game/1.06-revenue-phase.md#1.06.52)), (Rebel Maintenance: [1.06.1](../01-basic-game/1.06-revenue-phase.md#1.06.1))

**Local Taxes:** Taxes that can only be used for raising Provincial Armies/Fleets. ([2.02.2](../02-advanced-game/2.02-provincial-wars.md#2.02.2))

**Losing Conditions:** ([1.12.1](../01-basic-game/1.12-victory-conditions.md#1.12.1))

**Losses:** (Land: [1.10.61](../01-basic-game/1.10-combat-phase.md#1.10.61)), (Naval: [1.10.62](../01-basic-game/1.10-combat-phase.md#1.10.62)) (Commander Death: [1.10.7](../01-basic-game/1.10-combat-phase.md#1.10.7))

**Loyalty:** A number (LOY) that measures the degree of adherence a Senator has to his current situation. ([1.07.31](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.31)) (Persuasion Attempt Effects: [1.07.411](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.411)) (Statesmen Loyalty: [1.07.312](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.312))

### M

**Maintenance:** (Legions/Fleets: [1.06.53](../01-basic-game/1.06-revenue-phase.md#1.06.53)) (Rebel Legions/Fleets: [1.06.2](../01-basic-game/1.06-revenue-phase.md#1.06.2), [1.11.35](../01-basic-game/1.11-revolution-phase.md#1.11.35)), (Rebel Provincial Maintenance: [2.03.6](../02-advanced-game/2.03-rebel-governors.md#2.03.6))

**Major Prosecution:** A prosecution in which a guilty verdict results in death or banishment. ([1.09.413](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.413), [1.09.442](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.442))

**Manpower Shortage:** An Event indicating that Legions or Fleets cost twice as much to purchase by the Senate this turn.

**Master of Horse:** A Dictator's second-in-command. ([1.09.34](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.34))

**Matching Wars:** Wars that, when active together, double, triple, or quadruple in Strength. ([1.07.332](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.332)), (Imminent Wars: [1.07.3321](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.3321))

**Middle Republic Scenario:** A Scenario which uses the gray deck. (Setup: [3.02](../03-scenarios/3.02-middle-republic.md#3.02))

**Military Plans:** In a solitaire or two-player game, it is the 2nd of 3 stages within the Senate Phase where the military action plan is decided. ([4.05.3](../04-solitaire-two-player.md#4.05.3))

**Military rating:** A number (MIL) that represents a particular Senator's ability as a Commander of military Forces. ([1.07.31](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.31))

**Minor Motions:** Gratuitous proposals to increase the flavor of the Game. ([1.09.83](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.83))

**Minor Prosecution:** A Prosecution in which a guilty verdict results in the loss of Influence, Popularity, Prior Consul marker, and Concessions. ([1.09.412](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.412), [1.09.441](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.441))

**Mortality Chits:** Chits with a skull and numbers (1-30) that correspond to Senator ID numbers. These are drawn from an opaque cup/bag when Mortality Resolution is needed. ([1.05.21](../01-basic-game/1.05-mortality-phase.md#1.05.21))

**Mortality Phase:** ([1.05](../01-basic-game/1.05-mortality-phase.md#1.05))

**Mortality Resolution:** The process of drawing a Mortality Chit(s). The corresponding Senator (Family or Statesmen) matching the ID # of the Mortality Chit dies. ([1.05.21](../01-basic-game/1.05-mortality-phase.md#1.05.21), [1.05.3](../01-basic-game/1.05-mortality-phase.md#1.05.3))

### N

**Naval Battle:** A battle fought strictly between Fleets. ([1.10.12](../01-basic-game/1.10-combat-phase.md#1.10.12))

**Naval Interception:** When Rome attempts to intercept a Rebel Fleet. ([2.03.72](../02-advanced-game/2.03-rebel-governors.md#2.03.72))

**Naval Victory:** A marker put on a War to designate that the Naval Battle has already been won. ([1.10.12](../01-basic-game/1.10-combat-phase.md#1.10.12))

**Neutral:** A "dummy" Faction in a solitaire or two-player game. ([4.01.2](../04-solitaire-two-player.md#4.01.2))

**No Recruitment:** An Event indicating that Legions or Fleets may not be purchased by the Senate this turn.

**Nomination:** A type of Proposal. ([1.09.13](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.13)) (Cannot Decline: [1.09.172](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.172))

### O

**Oratory:** A number (ORA) that indicates the political skills and voting power of a Senator. ([1.07.31](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.31))

**Other Business:** Any business that can be conducted after vacant Provinces have been filled. ([1.09.6](../01-basic-game/1.09-senate-phase.md#1.09.6))

### P

**Personal Treasury:** Talents belonging to and residing on a single Senator. ([1.06.1](../01-basic-game/1.06-revenue-phase.md#1.06.1))

**Persuasion Attempts:** The attempt of one Senator to convince another to join a player's Faction. ([1.07.4](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.4)) (Resolution: [1.07.41](../01-basic-game/1.07-forum-phase.md#1.07.41))
